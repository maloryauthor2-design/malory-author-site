About the Book

In Soar, you pray to a god or you scrape by without one, and the gods take a cut of everything that moves. Most people pick a side and stick with it. The protagonist of The Soar Chronicles did neither, which is why he’s a Classless private investigator with a face that gets him hit a lot — and why, when a High Priestess turns up dead in her own temple, he’s the only person in the city nobody’s paid to silence.

What starts as a murder is, by chapter three, four murders. By chapter six it’s a question of which god is short a worshipper and which is gaining one. By the end, the answer matters less than what Soar is willing to do to keep the question quiet.

Audiobook performed by Neil Hellegers.

For Readers Who…

love the hardboiled rhythm of Raymond Chandler or the genre-bending detective work in Noobtown and The Land. Heavier on noir, mystery, and consequence than most LitRPG — you’re not here to grind, you’re here to figure out who did it.
