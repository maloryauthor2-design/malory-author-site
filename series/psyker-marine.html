Psyker Marine

Weaponised minds. Galactic war. The kind of combat-focused LitRPG that earns the word ‘relentless.’

AuthorJake Malory
Books6
AudiobookMatthew Michael Lewis & Renee Nolen

The Setting

James Thorne was a nobody until the aliens came. Psyker Marine (sold on retailers as the Galactic Invasion series) is the relentless military-LitRPG escalation that follows. Squad dynamics, hostile worlds, and a System that rewards exactly the kind of operator nobody wanted in the same room.

The series treats violence as craft. Every engagement has a specific shape — who has cover, who has range, what the System is rewarding, what it’s punishing. By book three most readers can predict Thorne’s first move because they’ve absorbed the same tactical grammar. That trained-up reading position is the engine of the genre. Six books and an omnibus carry the war from first contact to its final shape.

Tone

Tight, military, technically grounded. A combat LitRPG for readers who think of the System as a tactical interface rather than a wish-fulfilment generator.

Comparable Reads

Jason Anspach & Nick Cole’s Galaxy’s Edge; Rick Partlow’s Drop Trooper; Starship Troopers with a working stat sheet.
