# Malory — Brand Foundation

A working brand document for the Malory pen name. This file is the source of truth. The website CSS, the cover assets, the social templates, the press kit, and any future merch all pull from here.

Companion pen name: **Jake Malory** — used only for *Psyker Marine* and *Arcane Galaxy: Chaos Protocols*. Jake Malory is a sub-identity inside the Malory umbrella, not a separate brand. He gets a credit line, not a separate visual system.

---

## 1. Who Malory is

**Author of sci-fi, LitRPG, and progression fantasy.** Six active series across military sci-fi, post-apocalyptic LitRPG, space opera, military progression fantasy, and dark-fantasy adventure. PhD researcher in the day job. Writes the kind of genre fiction that men in their 30s–50s actually read on Kindle Unlimited at 11pm.

**Core promise to the reader:** the characters are competent, the stakes are real, the prose is dry and confident. Books deliver action, system mechanics, and emotional weight — in that order. No filler. No slop. The cover says "you know what this is" and the inside delivers.

**Imprint:** Operates as Malory directly — no separate imprint name. The author IS the brand.

---

## 2. Visual personality

Military. Functional. Confident. The brand reads like a **modern indie publishing house specialised in disciplined genre fiction** — a Baen back-catalogue crossed with a serious LitRPG operation. Field-manual typography, dark surfaces, signal colours used sparingly.

**Avoid:** generic fantasy gold filigree, romance pastels, neon LitRPG screen aesthetics, literary-monograph daintiness, anything that signals "AI-generated by default."

**Embrace:** dark backgrounds, terminal-density information, deliberate restraint, the feel of a piece of professional kit. The site should look like something a Special Forces sergeant would respect.

## 3. Color system

The current website palette refined into named roles. Every cover band, every social post, every brand surface pulls from this list and only this list.

| Role | Hex | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary deep** | `#0D1015` | Background base, dark cover bands, the canvas |
| Primary mid | `#1A1E24` | Surface 1 — card backgrounds, raised UI panels |
| Surface 2 | `#22272F` | Hover/active states, secondary surfaces |
| Line | `#333A44` | Hairline rules, panel borders |
| **Brand brass** | `#C9A85A` | Primary accent — author name, key headings, illuminated detail. Less bright than casino gold, more like aged hardware. |
| Brass bright | `#E0C478` | Highlights, hover states on brass elements |
| Brass deep | `#927838` | Pressed/active states, shadows |
| **Signal red** | `#D04848` | Warnings, "limited time" CTAs, deal alerts. Used sparingly — when it appears, it means something. |
| Cream | `#E8E0CC` | Body text on dark surfaces, light author-name on dark covers |
| Muted | `#9AA0A8` | Secondary copy, meta text, kicker labels |

**Cover Band tone:**

| Role | Hex | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| Cover band | `#0D1015` at 90% opacity over artwork | A fixed dark surface at the top or bottom of every cover where the **author name lockup** sits. Keeps "Malory" readable on any artwork. |

## 4. Typography

| Face | Role | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Fraunces** | Display + author lockup | Author name on covers, hero headings on website, social card headlines. The literary anchor. |
| **Newsreader** | Body serif | Long-form reading copy on essays and About pages. |
| **Inter** | UI / body sans | Nav, buttons, captions, anything that needs to read as instrument rather than prose. |
| **JetBrains Mono** | Mono | System messages, LitRPG status blocks, metadata, byline credits. The "this is operating data" register. |

**Author lockup spec:**
- Face: Fraunces, weight 600 (Semibold)
- Tracking: +0.04em
- Case: Mixed case ("Malory" not "MALORY")
- Color: `#E8E0CC` (cream) on dark band, OR `#0D1015` (primary deep) on light surface
- Position: Top or bottom band of cover, centered, with a thin brass underline rule (1px, 60% width)

**Note on Orbitron / Rajdhani:**
The current site has these as legacy variables. They are deprecated as of this document. New work uses the four faces above only. When refactoring CSS, the rule is: anywhere Orbitron currently lives, switch to Fraunces; anywhere Rajdhani lives, switch to Inter (or JetBrains Mono if it's a status/meta context).

## 5. Brand marks

### Three distinct marks, three distinct jobs:

**a) Malory Wordmark** — the everyday brand layer
- "Malory" in Fraunces 600, with the brass rule and the kicker
- Lives on every cover, social card, ad, signature, press kit cover
- Where personality won't fit, the wordmark goes
- File: `brand/malory-wordmark.svg`

**b) Malory Mark (monogram)** — the small-form identity
- M-glyph in brass on primary-deep, with a thin brass ring
- For favicon, social avatar, watermark, nav, anywhere ≤256px
- File: `brand/malory-mark.svg`

**c) Jake Malory secondary lockup** — for Psyker / Arcane Galaxy material only
- Same Fraunces face, but the "Jake" is set in a lighter weight (400) above "Malory" at 600
- Used on those two series' marketing material only — NOT on the umbrella site nav
- File: `brand/jake-malory-wordmark.svg`

## 6. Series identity layer

Each series gets a **color anchor** drawn from the brand palette plus a small set of approved supplementary tones. Used as the spine color, the social card accent strip, and the website series-card border. Allows readers to clock the series at a glance without changing cover art style.

| Series | Color anchor | Hex | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Psyker Marine** (military sci-fi) | Signal red | `#D04848` | Combat, blood, urgency. Reads as "fire when ready." |
| **Apocalypse on the Bounty** (post-apocalyptic LitRPG) | Brass | `#C9A85A` | Sand, rust, sun-bleached metal — the brand's anchor colour, because this is the flagship. |
| **Punish the System** (LitRPG) | Console green | `#3DA672` | System-prompt green. The terminal colour. Direct visual link to the LitRPG mechanics. |
| **Arcane Galaxy: Chaos Protocols** (space opera) | Electric blue | `#3D7FB8` | Cool, cosmic, scientific. Distinguishes the space-opera register from the military-sci-fi red. |
| **The Soar Chronicles** (military progression fantasy) | Steel | `#7A8B99` | Cold, hard, structural. Progression-fantasy in a martial register. |
| **Morgan and Merlin** (dark fantasy adventure) | Deep violet | `#5B3A7A` | Mythic, magical, older than the rest. Stands apart from the sci-fi/LitRPG cluster. |
| **Boys' Own Adventures** (short stories / anthologies) | Brass bright | `#E0C478` | Lighter brass for the anthology / short-form material — visually adjacent to Apocalypse on the Bounty but distinct. |

If/when a new series launches, its colour is picked from: the unused half of the brand palette, plus one approved tertiary. Document it here in the same commit.

## 7. Usage rules

**On book covers:**
- Author lockup always present at top OR bottom, on Cover Band tone
- Title typography is series-specific (each series has its own title face — Psyker uses one, Punish another) — that's intentional sub-genre signaling
- Series color anchor appears as a thin (4–6px) rule above or below the author lockup
- Cover art is the cover art — the brand layer sits on top, never fights it

**On social cards (1080×1080 / 1080×1920):**
- Author lockup top, series anchor strip bottom
- Background: brand palette gradient or solid `#0D1015`
- Brass for emphasis, signal red for deal/launch CTAs, never both at high intensity in the same composition
- Body copy in JetBrains Mono if it's "data" (numbers, system messages), Inter if it's promotional

**On the website:**
- Already largely locked. Future changes pull from this doc, not from designer intuition.
- The Human Authored badge stays — it's a values statement, not a brand element.
- Series-anchor colour appears on each series page as a top rule and on the homepage as the dot before each series name.

**Owned-channel rule:**
The two channels we promote prominently are **Substack** (the newsletter — `maloryauthor.substack.com`) and **Patreon** (when/if launched). Other platforms (Amazon, Audible, Goodreads) are linked but not promoted with their brand colour. Substack/Patreon get the brass treatment because they're the long-term reader relationship — third parties don't.

**Avoid:**
- The Human Authored badge larger than 25px tall
- The deprecated Orbitron / Rajdhani fonts in any new work
- Casino-gold (#FFD700, etc.) — we use brass (#C9A85A) instead
- Brass and signal red both at 100% saturation in the same composition
- Any colour outside this palette
