# Series Strategy — from views to follows (June 2026)

## The diagnosis
Five trials, good views, no follows. The reason is structural, not creative: **people follow shows, not jokes.** A one-off gag delivers its entire payoff in 11 seconds — the viewer has nothing left to follow FOR. Every reel so far has been a complete, self-contained joke with a generic "follow for more" that promises nothing specific.

The research (June 2026) is unambiguous:
- IG's 2026 ranking weights **multiple watches in one session** as a top distribution signal — episodic content compounds where one-offs can't, and a series trains the algorithm to redistribute future episodes to the same warm audience.
- Creators are coached to close each episode with **an unresolved promise of what's next** — that's the mechanic that drives profile visits and follows.
- Meta is mid-test on a **"Series" feature for Reels** (announced ~June 2, 2026): episodes bundled into a hub tab on the profile, viewers can save a series and pick up where they left off. The platform is literally building infrastructure for exactly this.
- Proof case: **Hannah Nicole Maehrer** (@hannahnicolemae) — episodic Villain/Assistant comedy skits with recurring characters and interconnected storylines built the audience that made *Assistant to the Villain* a bestseller. Recurring characters + episode format + follow-for-the-next-one. That's the model, in our genre-adjacent lane.

## The strategy: build a show
One named, numbered comedy series in the proven native-artifact style. Same skin every week, fresh canon every week.

**What changes vs the one-offs:**
1. **Series badge in the first frame** — "ep 4" signals a catalog exists. A cold viewer who laughs now has a reason to visit the profile (back-episodes) and follow (future episodes).
2. **The CTA names the next payoff.** Not "follow for more" — "follow for episode 5: [specific tease]." A dry one-line next-episode tease on the title card.
3. **A serial spine under the episodic gags.** Each episode is a self-contained joke (cold-viewer safe), but they accumulate toward something the audience can see coming — dramatic irony is the follow engine.
4. **Fixed cadence, fixed day.** Weekly. The show airs Thursdays.
5. **Fresh canon every episode — no gag reuse.** Solves the auto-share confusion problem at the root.
6. **Profile = the show's funnel:** bio names the show, episodes 1–3 pinned, covers use the episode badge so the grid reads as a season. TikTok: same episodes in a playlist (already supported). If Meta's Series tab reaches the account, bundle day one.

## How Trials fit now
- **Reused-gag format tests** (like the lock screen vs iMessage A/B): Trial with **auto-share OFF**. Followers never see it, no confusion, we still get the cold read.
- **Fresh episodes:** Trial with auto-share ON, or post normally once the format is proven — followers seeing every episode is the point of a show.

## Flagship candidates (pick one — one show per account)
**A. The Morgan show (M&M) — recommended.** Best cold performer (600/400 both M&M). Premise with a built-in spine: a modern woman is becoming THE Morgan le Fay, one week at a time, told through phone artifacts. Cold viewers know how the legend ends; watching her get there is the show. Episode = one fresh manuscript gag in the established skins (thread, lock screen, Wrapped, autocomplete, Wikipedia stub — the skin can rotate; the badge and characters are the constant).
**B. The Gazelle (AG).** Workplace sitcom in space, group-chat skin. Weakest cold result so far (200, 0 saves/shares) and Jake Malory pen-name complexity — hold unless A stalls.

## Metrics that matter now
Follows per 1k cold reach is THE metric (it was the goal all along; views were a proxy). Secondary: profile visits per 1k, back-episode watches after a trial, saves (save-a-series behavior). Screenshot 24h insights every episode.
