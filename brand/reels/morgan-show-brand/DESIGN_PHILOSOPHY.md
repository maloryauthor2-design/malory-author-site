# Dark Age Playbill — a design philosophy

A movement for marketing artifacts that borrow the grammar of the cinema one-sheet and play it completely straight in service of a joke. The form is reverent — the billing block, the critics' stars, the tagline in tracked capitals, the hero figure against a vast sky — and the reverence IS the comedy. Nothing on the surface winks. The craft must be so disciplined, so meticulously executed, that the absurdity of a five-star pull quote attributed to a dragon lands like a fact.

Space is vertical and theatrical. The upper field belongs to image and atmosphere: a painted figure, weather, distance — graded so the eye falls without interruption from sky to title. The lower field is architecture: a strict typographic stack in which every element knows its station — kicker above title, title above rule, rule above tagline, tagline above the fine print. The billing block is the soul of the form: condensed capitals, painstakingly spaced, small enough to whisper and dense enough to feel official.

Color obeys the house: deep near-black surfaces, cream display type, aged brass for the author's mark, one series accent used like a signature — a strip, a rule, never a flood. The painting supplies all the saturation the composition is permitted; the typography stays in candlelight. Grain unifies paint and pixel so the artifact reads as printed object, not screen export.

Hierarchy is delivered through scale contrast practiced at master level: enormous display set against six-point fine print with almost nothing in between. Text is minimal and load-bearing — every line either sells the premise or deepens the parody, and most lines do both. No element crowds another; the margins are generous because confidence is generous.

The finished work must look laboured over for days by a poster artist at the top of the field — optically centered, hand-kerned, the gradient breaking exactly where the eye expects a curtain. It should survive being printed, framed, and hung beside the real thing without apology.
